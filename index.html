Drone Specifications

  • Maximum diagonal wheelbase: 250mm
  • Maximum weight with battery: 1.5 kg
  • FPV (First Person View) racing allowed with standard 5.8GHz frequency
  • Propeller guards mandatory for safety
  • Must have functional failsafe system

Competition Format

  • Qualification round: Individual time trials (top 16 advance)
  • Knockout rounds: Head-to-head racing (single elimination)
  • Each pilot gets 2 minutes to complete the course
  • Fastest completion time wins; failure to finish = DNF
  • Collision with obstacles adds 5 second penalty per collision

Technical Requirements

  • All drones must pass pre-flight safety inspection
  • Video transmitter power: Maximum 600mW
  • Spare parts and batteries responsibility of team
  • No autonomous flight - manual control only
  • GPS and altitude hold features must be disabled

Safety Protocols

  • Pilots must maintain visual line of sight at all times
  • Arm drone only when instructed by race official
  • Immediate disarm upon crash or leaving course boundaries

Project Requirements

  • Must demonstrate original innovation in robotics/automation
  • Project should address real-world problem or need
  • Working prototype mandatory (simulation-only projects not accepted)
  • Maximum presentation time: 15 minutes + 10 minutes Q&A
  • Teams must submit project abstract (max 500 words) by March 1, 2025

Judging Criteria

  • Innovation & Creativity (30%): Uniqueness and originality of solution
  • Technical Complexity (25%): Engineering skills and technology integration
  • Functionality (25%): How well the project works and solves the problem
  • Real-world Impact (15%): Practical applicability and scalability
  • Presentation Quality (5%): Clarity and professionalism of demonstration

Submission Requirements

  • Project documentation including circuit diagrams, code, and build process
  • Bill of materials with cost breakdown
  • PowerPoint/PDF presentation (max 20 slides)
  • 2-minute demo video (optional but recommended)
  • All submissions due by March 10, 2025

Presentation Guidelines

  • Setup time: 10 minutes before presentation
  • Teams must bring their own equipment and power supplies
  • Internet connectivity not guaranteed - prepare offline demos
  • Projector and display screen will be provided
  • Live demonstrations highly encouraged

Intellectual Property

  • All projects must be original work of the team
  • Open-source components must be properly credited
  • Teams retain full IP rights to their innovations
